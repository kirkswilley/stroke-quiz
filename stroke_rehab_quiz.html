Stroke & Rehabilitation Quiz

1. Which type of stroke commonly is associated with visual impairments?

The posterior cerebral artery supplies the occipital lobe, which is associated with vision.

2. Which type of stroke commonly impacts language such as aphasia?

The left MCA supplies language areas like Broca’s and Wernicke’s.

3. What are modifiable risk factors to preventing a stroke from occurring?

Poor diet is modifiable; age, gender, and genetics are not.

4. The warning signs for stroke: BE FAST stand for?

BE FAST: Balance, Eyes, Face, Arms, Speech, Time/headache.

5. Which of the following are research benefits to Aquatic therapy post stroke?

Research supports B and C — improved balance and ROM.

6. According to research, which fitness methods help support postural control post stroke?

Both Pilates and HIIT are supported by research.

7. Which is an example of adding cognitive tasks while exercising?

Dual-tasking with cognitive recall enhances mental engagement.

8. Adaptation for client with grip difficulty during dumbbell work?

Use grips or straps — both are valid.

9. Which exercise helps to work on balance?

Standing marching challenges postural control and balance.

10. Progression for step ups?

Progress from small seated motion to unassisted standing step-up.

11. Why use the RPE scale?

RPE helps both monitor exertion and track change.

12. Correct BP position?

Proper BP: seated, supported, arm at heart level.

13. Client stands and becomes dizzy — first action?

Seating and monitoring is the safest immediate action.

14. Why assess before starting a workout?

You need a baseline to set safe, personalized goals.

15. What is neuroplasticity?

Neuroplasticity = ability to form new neural connections.

16. How to apply neuroplasticity in training?

Repetition and specificity reinforce brain pathways.

17. Can trainers use cognitive tasks?

Trainers can absolutely use cognitive dual tasks.

18. Client can only raise left arm to 90° — how to adapt?

Keep training both sides with left at safe ROM.

19. How to adjust a walker?

Walker handles = wrist crease, slight elbow bend.

20. Exercise to help get out of bed?

Bed mobility needs glute bridges, marches, press-ups.
